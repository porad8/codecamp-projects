Wangari Maathai

A pioneer in linking environmental protection with human rights.

The following is a short timeline of Wangari Maathai's life.

  • 1940 - Born in Nyeri, Kenya.
  • 1951 - After a short stint at Ihithe Primary School in her village, Wangari joined St. Cecilia's Intermediate Primary School in Nyeri where she converted to Catholicism.
  • 1956 - Joined Loreto High School in Limuru,Kenya (the only Catholic High school for girls in Kenya at the time).
  • 1960 - Received a scholarship to study at Mount St. Scolastica College (now Benedictine College) in Kansas, USA, where she majored in biology.
  • 1964 - Joined University of Pittsburgh for a master's degree in biology.
  • 1966 - Appointed as a research assistant at University College of Nairobi (now University of Nairobi).
  • 1967 - Started to pursue a doctorate at both University of Giessen and University of Munich in Germany.
  • 1969 - Marries Mwangi Mathai.
  • 1971 - Becomes the first East African woman to receive a Ph.D.
  • 1974 - Joins the local board of Environment Liaison Center.
  • 1977 - Establishes the Green Belt Movement (GBM) which encouraged women to plant tree nuseries with a small stipend for each seedling later plantes elsewhere. GBM was acclaimed worlwide for it's success in forest conservation, education, advocacy of human rights, democratisation of access to public lands, environmental justice, and women's economic empowerment.
  • 1982 - Joined politics and campaigned for the Parliamentary seat in her rural home but was disqualified and later lost her job at the University of Nairobi.
  • 1991 - Receives the Goldman Environmental Prize and the Africa Prize for Leadership by Hunger Project.
  • 1993 - Receives the Edinburgh Medal in Scotland and the Jane Addams International Women's Leadership Award.
  • 1997 - Run for president but lost due to strong intimidation from the government.
  • 1999 - Maathai instigates the start of the famous Karura Forest protests.
  • 2002 - Started teaching as the Dorothy McCluskey Visiting Fellow for Conservation at the Yale University's School of Forestry and Environmental Studies. Was also elected to the Kenyan Parliament and later appointed assistant minister in the Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources.
  • 2003 - Founded the Mazingira Green Party of Kenya, a member of the Federation of Green Parties of Africa and Global Greens.
  • 2004 - Awarded the 2004 Nobel Peace Prize for her contribution to sustainable development, democracy and peace
  • 2011 - Dies of complications arising from ovarian cancer while receiving treatment at a Nairobi hospital.
